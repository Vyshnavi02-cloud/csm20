4.Problem Statement

In many regions, citizens face significant difficulties in reporting public issues such as infrastructure damage, sanitation problems, electricity failures, public safety concerns, and corruption. Existing grievance redressal systems are often manual, fragmented, or poorly managed, resulting in delayed responses, lack of transparency, and minimal accountability from concerned authorities. Complaints may be ignored, misplaced, or not properly tracked, leaving citizens unaware of the status of their submissions. Additionally, there is no effective mechanism to verify the authenticity of complaints or ensure that unresolved issues are escalated to higher authorities within a defined timeframe.

The absence of a centralized, secure, and intelligent platform reduces public trust in governance systems and limits effective citizen participation. Therefore, there is a need to develop a digital, transparent, and automated grievance redressal system that enables citizens to submit verified complaints directly to appropriate authorities, track progress in real time, and ensure timely resolution through structured escalation and monitoring mechanisms.
